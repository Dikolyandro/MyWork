History and Culture

Germany has a deep historical and cultural heritage, from the medieval castles of Bavaria to the modern art scenes of Berlin. It has produced world-renowned composers like Beethoven and Bach, as well as influential philosophers such as Kant and Nietzsche.

Science and Innovation

Germany is a global leader in science and technology. It is the birthplace of the automobile and home to cutting-edge research institutions. German engineering is synonymous with quality and precision.

Quick Facts

  • Berlin is not only the capital of Germany, but also its largest city and a major cultural and political center.

  • German is the most widely spoken language in Germany, and it is also one of the major languages in Europe.

  • Germany adopted the Euro in 2002, replacing the Deutsche Mark, and it is now part of the European Union's monetary system.

  • Germany is known for Oktoberfest (a major beer festival), the Autobahn (famous for stretches with no speed limits), and the scenic Black Forest region.
